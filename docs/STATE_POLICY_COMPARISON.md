# State-Level Incentive Policies — Comparison, Differences, Gaps

*Synthesized 2026-07-23 from the twin's verified layers: **layer 12** (state catalog,
site-verified instruments, 30 states/UTs), **layer 13** (flat index: 117 state
instruments of 312 total), **layer 18** (state incentive monitor: disbursal/arrears
evidence, built 2026-07-21), **layer 31** (Invest India state-page policy carousels).
Every claim below traces to a layer record; this page is a reading of the data, not a
new sweep.*

---

## 1. The common architecture

Nearly every industrial state runs the same two-tier design:

- **An umbrella fiscal policy** — capital or SGST-linked subsidy graded by investment
  size, plus stamp-duty/electricity-duty exemptions and land concessions:
  UP IIEPP-2022 · Gujarat Aatmanirbhar-2022 · TN Industrial Policy 2021 structured
  packages · Karnataka IP 2025-30 (notified 2025-02-08) · Rajasthan RIPS-2024 ·
  MP Investment Promotion Scheme 2025 · AP IDP 4.0 (2024-29) · Punjab IBDP-2022 ·
  Maharashtra PSI-2019 → **MIISP-2025 transition**.
- **Sector verticals** layered on top — where states actually differ (§2).

Because umbrella texts converge, the real decision variables for an investor are
**(a) central-stacking top-up size, (b) arrears record, (c) published-disbursal
transparency** — which is why layer 18 tracks payment evidence, not policy prose.

### Central-stacking generosity — the sharpest differentiator

Verified on-page in four states (layer 10/12; legal basis for stacking = MeitY PLI FAQ
Clause 3.6):

| State | Top-up rule |
|---|---|
| **Uttar Pradesh** | ECMS top-up **equal to the central grant** — a doubling |
| **Assam** | **+40%** on ISM (semiconductor) central incentive |
| **Andhra Pradesh** | **10% of the PLI** amount as state top-up |
| **Kerala** | **20%** top-ups on eligible central schemes |

The same ECMS/ISM project receives materially different total stacks by state.

### Design philosophy

- **UP**: base capital subsidy 10–30% of eligible investment paid over **10–20 years**,
  graded Large (>₹50 cr) → Mega (₹200–500 cr) → Super-Mega (₹500–3,000 cr).
- **Gujarat / Rajasthan**: SGST-reimbursement models (Haryana HEEP: 30–100% SGST).
- **Tamil Nadu**: bespoke **structured packages** negotiated via Guidance TN for
  Mega/Super-Mega/Ultra-Mega projects.
- **MP**: disburses **on-stage at investor conclaves** (₹270 cr + ₹425 cr at a single
  event; claims legacy liabilities cleared).

## 3. The delivery divide (the real difference)

Policy text converges; **payment behaviour diverges** (layer 18, public-record sweep):

### Arrears states
| State | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Telangana | **₹3,736 cr confirmed arrears** (BRS exit, Dec-2023); overlapping counts to >₹4,500 cr since 2016-17 |
| Andhra Pradesh | ₹3,000–5,000 cr pending; YSRCP-era disbursal ~₹2,000 cr over 5 years |
| Punjab | "₹150 cr disbursed in Q1 FY26 **exceeded the prior two years combined**" — an implicit arrears admission |
| J&K (NCSS, central-sector) | ~₹83 cr approved in FY24 against a ₹28,400 cr outlay; documented industry delay complaints |

### Transparency states
| State | Practice |
|---|---|
| Rajasthan | Press-releases annual RIPS disbursals (**₹765.78 cr FY25, +293% YoY**) — best-in-class |
| Gujarat | Publishes MSME-track (₹7,864 cr/5y; ₹245 cr to ~2,400 enterprises under Aatmanirbhar) but **login-gates the Large/Mega dashboard** |
| Maharashtra, Karnataka | **No annual disbursal series published at all** |
| Haryana, West Bengal | No findable aggregate outgo in budget docs, CAG extracts, or press |

### Outlier
- **Odisha**: budgets only **₹15 cr/yr** for IPR-2022 subsidies against a
  **₹24,823 cr MoU pipeline** — a two-orders-of-magnitude promise/provision gap.

## 4. Gaps

1. **Catalog coverage vs reality**: green hydrogen and data-centre verticals show only
   2 states each in the verified catalog while EV shows 25. Partly real thinness,
   partly a layer-12 capture gap — TN/Telangana/Maharashtra DC policies are known to
   exist but were not site-verified into the catalog. *The layer-31 Invest India
   state-page PDF carousels (e.g. Gujarat exposes 15+ named policy PDFs) are the ready
   feed to close this.*
2. **Disbursal-data gap**: 7 of 15 monitored states carry **UNVERIFIED** aggregate
   disbursals (AP, Odisha, WB, Haryana, Assam-IIPA, and others) — absence of published
   outgo is recorded as a finding, not skipped.
3. **Policy-transition windows open right now**: Maharashtra (PSI-2019 closed to new
   units; MIISP-2025 implementation rules pending) and Haryana (HEEP-2020 expires
   2025-12-31; successor awaited).
4. **East-side thinness**: WB (Banglashree is MSME-scale; ₹1,229 cr FY26 budget line),
   Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, Bihar carry 2–3 verified instruments each vs Tamil Nadu's
   and Telangana's 9 — the incentive map mirrors the existing industrial map rather
   than correcting it. Odisha's GO-EAST amendments (IPR-2022, 2025-26: extra
   incentives for 15 under-industrialized districts) are the explicit counter-move.
